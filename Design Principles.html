Core Deign Principals
Simplicity Consistency Accessibility
  • Keep it simple and avoid unnecessary complexity
  • Strive for clarity and avoid clutter
  • Use whitespace and typography effectively to create hierarchy and focus
  • Avoid overloading users with too much information or too many options
  • Make sure the design is intuitive and easy to understand
  • Consider the context and user's goals when designing for simplicity
  • Maintain consistency in visual and interaction design across the product or system
  • Use consistent patterns, elements, and language to create a sense of familiarity and predictability
  • Ensure consistency across different platforms and devices
  • Provide clear and consistent feedback to users to avoid confusion or frustration
  • Continuously test and iterate on design to maintain consistency
  • Ensure the design is accessible to users with disabilities and diverse needs
  • Follow web accessibility guidelines such as WCAG 2.1
  • Use high contrast colors and provide alternative text for images and other visual content
  • Provide keyboard navigation and other alternative input methods
  • Test the design with assistive technology and real users with disabilities
User-centered Aesthetics Performance
  • Design with the user in mind and prioritize their needs and goals
  • Conduct user research to understand user needs and behaviors
  • Create personas and user journeys to guide the design process
  • Test the design with real users to get feedback and iterate
  • Use data and analytics to inform design decisions and measure success
  • Create visually appealing designs that are engaging and memorable
  • Use color, typography, and other visual elements to create a consistent and attractive design
  • Consider the brand and overall tone and voice of the product or system
  • Create a unique and recognizable visual identity
  • Use design to evoke emotion and create a positive user experience
  • Optimize the design for speed and performance
  • Use best practices for web and mobile performance such as lazy loading and caching
  • Minimize the number of HTTP requests and reduce page load times
  • Use responsive design and optimize for different screen sizes and devices
  • Test the design with real users on different networks and devices to ensure optimal performance.
